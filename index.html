Bright Kwaku Boateng | WDD130

My name is Bright Kwaku Boateng, a 39-year-old Ghanaian with a deep appreciation for hard work, culture, and adventure. I currently work 12-hour shifts at Sentuo Oil Refinery, where I bring dedication and resilience to every task. When I’m not at the refinery, I retreat to my farm—my personal haven—where I reconnect with nature and cultivate the land with care. I’m also pursuing a degree in Software Engineering through BYU Pathway, driven by a passion for technology and a desire to build solutions that make life better. Balancing work, study, and farm life isn’t easy, but I thrive on the challenge and the growth it brings. Culturally, I’m rooted in Ghana’s vibrant spirit. I love Hip-life music—it’s the rhythm of my soul and a soundtrack to many of my days. My favorite dish is eba with palaba sauce, a meal that always reminds me of home and heritage. Beyond the everyday, I’m someone who dreams big. I love swimming and would love to try skydiving one day—because life is meant to be felt deeply and lived boldly. Japan is high on my travel list, with its rich traditions, futuristic cities, and serene landscapes calling to me. I’m a man of many layers: a worker, a student, a farmer, a dreamer. And I believe that with grit and grace, anything is possible

Ghana

Ghana is a country located on the Gulf of Guinea in West Africa. It is relatively small in area and population, but is celebrated for its rich history. The capital of Ghana is the coastal city of Accra. The country is multiethnic, with inhabitants including the Akan, Mossi, Ewe, and Ga-Adangme peoples. Ghana spans diverse ecologies, from coastal savannas to tropical rain forests. It is one of the most stable and democratic countries in West Africa.
